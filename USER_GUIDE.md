## 🛠️ Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1. Initial Selfie Scanning

1. Open the app and tap **Get Started** or tap the **Camera Button** on the Home dashboard.
2. Select **Camera** to capture a fresh selfie, or **Gallery** to load an existing photo.
3. Position your face in the center of the frame and confirm the image.
4. The on-device **Google ML Kit Face Detector** will locate your face and automatically crop it with appropriate padding to prepare for processing.

### 2. Fine-Tuning with Self-Analysis Studio

If you want to manually calibrate your results:

1. Tap the **Artist Palette Icon (🎨)** in the top-right corner of the **Style Report** page.
2. This opens the **Self-Analysis Palette Picker** where you will see three target droppers: **Skin**, **Hair**, and **Eye**.
3. **Draggable Droppers:** Tap any selector (Skin, Hair, or Eye) at the bottom, then drag its crosshair target (+) on the screen directly over the representative spot on your face.
4. **Color Draping:** Tap the **Hanger Icon** in the top-right of your selfie. This toggles a virtual fabric collar drape under your chin:
   - Tap any swatch under **Best Colors** or **Colors to Avoid** at the bottom to dye the fabric drape that color in real-time.
   - Drag the fabric drape vertically to align it perfectly under your chin.
   - Note: The crosshair droppers automatically hide when draping is active for a clean look!
5. Tap the **Load Palette Icon (⚙️ / edit)** next to your calculated season to override it and choose manually from the classic **12 Seasonal Subcategories** (e.g. Deep Autumn, Clear Spring).
6. Tap **Save Profile** to lock in your custom palette.

### 3. Managing Your Virtual Closet

1. Swipe to the **My Closet** tab from the main screen.
2. Tap **Add Garment** (or the floating `+` button).
3. Take a clean photo of your clothing item or select it from your gallery.
4. The AI analyzer will automatically extract the dominant fabric color, cluster it, and tag it with a user-friendly name (e.g., _Mustard Yellow_ or _Navy Blue_).
5. Categorize the item (Tops, Bottoms, Outerwear, Shoes, Accessories) and tap **Add to Closet**.

### 4. Using the Outfit Combinator

1. Select the **Outfit Combinator** tab from the main screen.
2. Choose the categories you want to match (e.g., Top + Bottom, or Top + Outerwear + Bottom).
3. The system will measure color spaces and present complete outfit combinations compiled from your own wardrobe.
4. Read the **Match Percentage** (e.g., _94% Match_) to verify how closely the outfit aligns with your natural personal color palette.

### 5. Real-Time Shopping Color Matcher

When shopping in physical stores or viewing clothes:

1. Tap the **Live Matcher** tab (camera finder icon) on the main dashboard.
2. Align the central target reticle over the garment fabric.
3. The screen will display the real-time match percentage against your personal palette:
   - **✨ Excellent Palette Match (86%+)**: Buy with absolute confidence.
   - **👍 Great Match (66% - 85%)**: High compatibility.
   - **⚠️ Poor Match**: Clashing color. Tap the screen to read custom **Synergy Tips** explaining how to wear it safely (e.g., wear it on the lower body, or offset it with a high-match scarf or jewelry).

## ✅ Do's & ❌ Don'ts for Maximum Accuracy

### 📸 Selfie Captures

- **✅ DO** take your selfie in **natural, indirect sunlight** (near a window is best).
- **✅ DO** pull your hair back so it doesn't shade your face or overlap your cheeks.
- **❌ DON'T** use artificial yellow lighting, heavy colored filters, or camera flashes—these warp your natural undertones.
- **❌ DON'T** wear heavy makeup (foundation, blush) when scanning, as it masks your natural skin undertones.
- **❌ DON'T** drag the Skin dropper to shadow creases (like nose edges or eye sockets) or bright highlights (like sweat glares). Keep it on flat cheeks, forehead, or chin.

### 👚 Closet Scanning

- **✅ DO** take photos of your clothes laid flat against a **solid, neutral background** (like a plain white bedsheet, wall, or floor).
- **✅ DO** ensure the room is brightly lit so the AI extracts the true color of the fabric.
- **❌ DON'T** take clothes photos while wearing them if other skin/background colors are visible in the frame, as this may throw off the color clustering engine.
- **❌ DON'T** scan heavily patterned multi-color fabrics expecting a single color match; flat, solid colors or dominant colors work best.

## 💡 Pro Tips for a Better Experience

1. **Adjust the Drape Vertically:** When using the color draping feature, use your finger to slide the drape up or down. Placing the collar line directly under your chin gives the best visual simulation of fabric lighting reflections on your jawline.
2. **AI Voice Assistant:** If you want your styling reports read aloud, head to your **Profile** page, look under **App Settings**, and toggle **AI Voice Assistant** to **ENABLED**. The assistant will read out occasion advice and color descriptions automatically.
3. **Use the 12-Season Manual Selector:** The automated algorithm categorifies you into the four broad seasons (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter). To lock in a more specialized palette, use the edit icon next to the calculated season in the Self-Analysis screen and pick your exact subcategory (e.g. Warm Spring, Cool Summer, Deep Winter).
